# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primarily recruiters and hiring managers evaluating Azhar Mahmood for senior full-stack / DevOps roles, with a secondary audience of startup founders, potential clients, and engineering peers. They arrive with limited time and high skepticism, often from a LinkedIn or GitHub link, frequently on mobile. Their job-to-be-done is to quickly judge whether this person is credible, senior, and worth a conversation, then form a lasting impression of the work.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio site that showcases Azhar's experience, projects, and case studies (per-company deep dives under `[...slug]`). It exists to convert a quick visit into a strong impression of craft and seniority. Success is a visitor leaving genuinely impressed by both the work and the execution of the site itself, the site is a live proof-of-skill, not just a list of facts.

## Brand Personality

Bold, modern, confident. The voice is self-assured and direct without bragging, let the work and the execution carry the weight. The site should feel like it was built by someone who sweats the details: motion-rich, alive, and memorable, while still reading as professional and senior. Energetic and expressive, never timid.

## Anti-references

- Generic resume-template portfolios (the biggest thing to avoid): cookie-cutter sections, stock layouts, a wall of skill badges with no point of view.
- Anything that looks like it came from a "developer portfolio starter."
- Static, lifeless pages with no personality or motion.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the proof.** Execution quality is itself the strongest credential, every interaction should reinforce "this person is excellent."
2. **Impress on first scroll.** Win skeptical, time-pressed visitors fast; the hero and first transition must land immediately.
3. **Motion with intent.** Lean into rich, expressive animation and motion as a signature, but every effect should feel deliberate and polished, never gratuitous jitter.
4. **Confident, not loud.** Bold and modern, but always professional, the kind of bold that reads as senior, not as a showreel.
5. **Substance behind the spectacle.** Real projects, real case studies, real depth; the flash must be backed by credible work.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Accessibility is intentionally deprioritized in favor of a rich, animated, "flying objects" experience, this is an explicit owner preference. Do not strip or gate animations for reduced-motion by default. The one hard constraint: it must always look professional and impressive. Keep text legible (sensible contrast and sizing) so the polish reads as senior, but motion-reduction and strict WCAG conformance are not requirements for this project.
